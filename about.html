Who We Are

About NSEE

NSEE — Núcleo de Sistemas Eletrônicos Embarcados — is an engineering research nucleus at Mauá Institute of Technology, developing embedded systems applied to space instrumentation, artificial intelligence, astrophysics, and scientific telescope software.

We build real-world solutions in partnership with international agencies and institutions such as ESA, DLR, GMTO, and FMABC — delivering software, simulators, and data science tools used in active space missions.

Our work is driven by technical rigour, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the ambition to place IMT at the frontier of global space and AI research.

Our Pillars

What Drives Us

Space Technology

Space camera and spectrometer simulators for ESA missions, hardware virtualisation, and ground station infrastructure.

AI & Data Science

Machine learning applied to oncology, assisted reproduction, ECG interpretation, and causal inference in health data.

Astrophysics

Black hole mass determination, James Webb data analysis, exoplanet detection, and galactic nucleus classification.

Giant Magellan Telescope

Software and graphical interfaces for GMT subsystem monitoring, 3D visualisation of mirror data, and computational support for GMTO.

Our History

Key Milestones

2003

CoRoT Satellite Emulator — OBSPM

Vanderlei Parro joins OBSPM (Paris) as a CAPES post-doctoral visiting researcher to work on emulation for the CoRoT space satellite.

2006

1st FAPESP Regular Grant — CoRoT

FAPESP Regular grant awarded for filtering ground-segment data for the CoRoT mission.

2007

French Cooperation & Early SimuCam Prototype

Partnership with CNES (Toulouse) and one-month FAPESP research stay in France. An IMT student develops the first camera simulator — the prototype that would become SimuCam.

2008

CNRS Collaboration & the PLATO Idea

CNRS funds a research visit to France to explore new cooperative projects after CoRoT. The idea of applying the simulator work to ESA's PLATO mission emerges here.

2009

CubeSats (AEB) & First PLATO Funding (CNPq)

AEB approves a CubeSat monitoring project (turtle tracking). Simultaneously, CNPq approves the first funding grant dedicated to the PLATO mission.

2010

PLATO–SimuCam Cooperation with OBSPM

French researchers visit IMT for a PLATO seminar, consolidating the bilateral collaboration that would drive SimuCam development.

2011

First Official SimuCam Delivery to France

IMT students and faculty present the first official SimuCam deliverable to OBSPM in France — a landmark milestone for the SpaceWire-based camera simulator.

2013

DLR & ESA Join SimuCam; CITAR Project

DLR requests a customised SimuCam application, broadening the European partnership. Simultaneously, the CITAR project launches via FINEP with CTI, INPE, AEB, IFUSP, IEAv, and FEI.

2015

1st Brafitec-CAPES, Double Degree & NIRPS Instrument

CAPES approves the 1st Brafitec project (SEAC) led by Mauá with École des Mines de Saint-Étienne, including industry involvement and a full double-degree programme. NSEE is also invited to collaborate on the NIRPS instrument for ESO's VLT telescopes.

2016

ELT — HIRES Instrument (ESO / Florence)

In cooperation with the University of Florence, NSEE participates in the Phase-B defence of the HIRES (now ANDES) instrument for ESO's Extremely Large Telescope. The contribution is recognised as instrumental to the instrument's approval.

2017

First International Course for EMSE Students

First international course offered to École des Mines de Saint-Étienne students during Mauá's Technology Week, covering embedded control systems.

2018

Giant Magellan Telescope — First Collaboration

NSEE begins its first GMT project in cooperation with Carnegie Observatories (Pasadena, CA). A FAPESP and GMTO post-doctoral grant is awarded for the project.

2019

Carnegie Contract Concluded & SimuCam–LESIA Integration

The Carnegie project concludes and a three-year GMT contract is initiated. In January, the first SimuCam integration session with the LESIA team takes place at IMT.

2020

CDIA Area Created — Public Health

NSEE creates the Data Science & Artificial Intelligence (CDIA) research area, initially focused on public health applications, including circadian risk analysis in partnership with Swansea University.

2021

Harvard Partnership — AI in Health (USD 500k)

Following a USD 500,000 research funding agreement, NSEE begins collaborating with Harvard University. The ConeCta-SP cancer survival project and an ECG deep-learning programme with Beneficência Portuguesa involve 20+ researchers.

2022

Final SimuCam Delivery — Four Countries

Engineers Rodrigo França and Daniel Gueter complete the final SimuCam delivery, visiting all partner institutions across Italy (INAF), Germany (DLR), France (OBSPM), and Austria (IWF).

2023

NASA/JPL Invitation, 1st BSATA & Nature Publication

NSEE is invited to participate in NASA's Veritas and EnVision missions through the DLR relationship. The first BSATA event gathers key partners. A colorectal cancer CDIA paper is published in Nature.

2024

EnVision Mission Formalised & 2nd GMT Contract

NSEE officially joins ESA's EnVision mission (Venus) with DLR and Max Planck. Prof. Roberto Menezes joins the mission science team. A second GMT software contract worth USD 140,000 is signed.

2025

2nd Brafitec-CAPES & CentraleSupélec Partnership

A second Brafitec project is approved in partnership with CentraleSupélec, benefitting 12 Mauá students and 36 Brazilians in total.
